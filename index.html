One major thing that you as a business owner and us as a development team must focus heavily on is Responsive Web Design, also known as mobile friendly design. According to TechCrunch, by the year 2020, there will be a staggering 6.1 billion Smartphone users. Which makes it pretty obvious that a very large amount of your web traffic would come from someone on a Mobile phone, but according to Hosting Tribunal the amount of traffic that is from a mobile device is 90%!! With numbers like that it's extrodinarily important to make sure your website doesn't just look good on a desktop, but a mobile phone as well.

Mobile Responsive design is extremely important, but that's not all when it comes to the layout of your website. Clients also want to know about what your company does and what product you are selling. Studies show that 86% of visitors who land on a conpany's webside homepage say they want to see information about the company's products and services. Ease of navigation is one thing but properly presenting what it is that you do is the most important thing.

A poor UI/UX design can leave potential clients questioning the quality of your business or, even worse, not knowing what it is that you are selling or what kind of law you specialize in. The last thing you want is for a client to struggle when, lets face it, they're most likely going through a difficult time as it is.
